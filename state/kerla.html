Kerala Cuisine

Surrounded by the sea, Kerala is home to numerous lip-smacking dishes prepared out of seafood, some of which include mussels, crab, tiger prawns, king prawns, tiny prawns, oysters, sardines, mackerel, tuna and gorgeous red lobsters. One just cannot stop salivating when walking the streets of Kerala. Unlike its neighbours, Kerala takes a lot of pride in its long list of non-vegetarian food that the state has to offer to its perpetually hungry tourists!

1. Erissery Recipe - Kerala Mathanga Vanpayar Erissery

Erissery or Mathanga Erissery is a traditional and popular recipe that is made for Onam Sadhya in Kerala. A sadhya is a lavish lunch spread of various vegetarian and vegan dishes served on a plantain leaf. Erissery recipe is made with yellow pumpkin and red cow peas cooked in a spiced coconut paste.Mathanga vanpayar erissery is a light and nutritious dish that makes for a wholesome and well-balanced lunch spread. This Kerala style of erissery is a no onion no garlic recipe that can be made on auspicious days like fasting and on Onam.

Erissery Ingredients:

• Red cow peas: This is called ‘vanpayar’ in Malayalam and ‘sigappu karamani’ in Tamil. This adds much-needed protein and fibre to this dish. Tempering: The tempering made of mustard seeds, red chillies, curry leaves and coconut in coconut oil enhances the taste and flavour of the erissery.

• Spice paste: The base for the erissery comes from the paste that we make using coconut, green chillies, red chillies and cumin seeds. This adds spice and creamy consistency to the erissery. Other ingredients: turmeric powder, sugar and salt to taste.

How to make Mathanga Erissery ?

Step 1:

1. Rinse red cow peas and soak overnight in water.

Step 2:

2. Pressure cook the cow peas for 3 whistles and set them aside.

Step 3:

3. Take all the ingredients for the paste in a mixie jar.

Step 4:

4. Add little water and grind to a fine paste and set it aside.

Step 5:

5. Take pumpkin, salt, turmeric powder, and sugar in a saucepan and cook till they are firm and soft.

Step 6:

6. Mash the pumpkin pieces with the back of a ladle.

Step 7:

7. Add cooked red cow peas, and ground coconut paste and mix well.

Step 8:

8. Cook for 3 to 4 mins over medium flame.

Step 9:

9. Make a tempering with mustard seeds, chillies, curry leaves and coconut in coconut oil.

Step 10:

10. Pour the tempering over the mathanga erissery and mix well. Serve hot.
